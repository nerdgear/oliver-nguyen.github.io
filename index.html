Welcome Darlings

Introduction

Welcome to my silly little site, it’s a mess and I have a lot of interests. I’m currently pursuing a gender-neutral honorific as a graduate student in Dr. Gretchen Daily’s Lab, housed in Stanford’s Biology Department, which lies on the unceded lands of the Muwekma Ohlone, who continually fight for their right to be recognized federally and receive benefits. All of which to say, I did not choose the alternative path of the gender-euphoric title, Chef, simply because I do not have enough prowess or coordination to make it in the industry.

What do I do?

Right now I’d consider myself an urban ecologist, seeing as I study mostly urban systems including urban wildlife communities, restorative community greenspaces, and harmful legacies of historical and contemporary urban policies. You don’t realize it as a kid but when you grow up and see where most of the places you’ve been living in has been redlined, it makes sense why your neighborhood is called the “projects” and John Smith Jr. on the other side of the tracks has a big treehouse in a large old maple tree. I still think our neighborhood was cooler though.

I’ve always enjoyed incorporating art into science so you’ll see some comics, doodles, and other visualizations of sorts sprinkled throughout my work. I never would have gotten into science reading boring old publications locked behind a paywall, so naturally I figured most other people wouldn’t either.

A Philosophy?

Sometimes I do some science, like working with large datasets and struggling to navigate through poison oak and blackberry thorns in the field. I love to code and create interactive maps, tinkering around the intersection of traditional and digital art has been exciting. I also am not very good at coming up with my own questions and often squander resources– which is why I tend to focus on local community and justice-based research (CBR).
